About Ace

Ace Ryder writes survival fiction that treats danger like a character — quiet, inevitable, and personal. Minimalist prose. Maximal stakes. A background in systems and human behavior shapes every page.

Voice

Lean, cinematic, grounded in reality. Violence as consequence, not spectacle.

Themes

Trust, scarcity, group dynamics, the cost of leadership — and what remains when the noise is gone.

Influences

McCarthy’s restraint, McLean’s momentum, Fincher’s control, Villeneuve’s silence.

Feature — Book One

No One Left But Us is a survival thriller set after a systems failure that erases convenience overnight. It’s not about the end of the world — it’s about what it costs to keep one.
